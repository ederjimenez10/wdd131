Caracas, Venezuela

Venezuela is the 33rd largest country in the world, spanning over 916,000 square kilometers across the northern coast of South America. It is home to nearly 10% of the Earth's biodiversity, including more than 1,400 bird species and 21,000 types of vascular plants. The nation is best known for Angel Falls—the world's tallest waterfall at 979 meters—the planet's largest proven oil reserves (303 billion barrels), and a Guinness World Record-holding 250 lightning flashes per square kilometer each year.
